Design

The blanket/jacket convertible will be able to thermoregulate for the user's body based on their specific needs. We will create a bilayer of agar bioplastic that will encase a Cellulose Nanofibril Aerogel phase changing composite that will act as a thermoregulating textile. We chose the aerogel as our inner material due to its low thermal conductivity, the environmentally sustainable aspect of organic phase-changing materials, and its potential for thermal responsiveness. Aerogels are materials with properties that are useful for flexible, lightweight insulation materials (Devi et al. 2019). Aerogels are suitable for thermal insulation due to their microporosity. Very small pore diameters limit airflow which then can trap heat efficiently (Caps & Fricke, 2004). Phase change materials (PCM's) release or absorb heat when there is a change in phase (e.g., solid to liquid, liquid to gas) (Kashyap et al., 2020) Because PCMs have poor thermal conductivity and are susceptible to leakage during a phase change, phase change composites (PCCs) are used as a composition of filler material for PCMs. These composites are constructed to achieve the desired properties of the material. Graphene in particular has been shown to have a high thermal conductivity (2000 - 4000 W/m K) making them a good candidate for creating a thermally conductive PCC. When graphene is in aerogel form, it acts as a conductive filler and form stabilizer, used typically in thermal energy storage systems as latent heat storage (Kashyap et al., 2020). Due to these findings, we wanted to explore the use of cellulose aerogel as a viable option for the filling of our transformable as it is a viable PCC and is similar to graphene aerogel. We plan to mirror the creation of this aerogel in Yang's study (2015), and have used both the research and list of materials to guide our own experimental process.

Moreover, based on our user research, we decided to create a blanket that can transform into a jacket and vice versa. The purpose of the blanket form is to retain heat while creating a feeling of protection for trauma patients (Graham Medical, 2018). The jacket provides a daily wearable option while still providing prevention from hypothermia. It is important for users to have these two options as traumatic events and hypothermia-inducing situations happen unexpectedly.

Human Impact

Our product will impact the lives of users, non-users, medical workers, and manufacturers. Our user group, trauma patients with hypothermia, will be positively impacted by this product as it provides a solution that immediately reacts to their medical needs. Once they have access to this blanket/jacket, they also have a preventive method of protecting themselves. Moreover, for users who have had traumatic experiences in the past, this product can empower them by providing wearable protection and ease of mind. Those considered to be outside of our user group can also benefit from this product as it can be used anytime as a wearable with prevention from general coldness. On the other hand, its impact on medical workers, a different type of user, would be reducing human error in providing medical support and quickly eliminating hypothermia as a diagnosis in a trauma patient. With providing this product to a trauma patient, a first response medical worker will be able to focus on other life saving measures and attend to others who are more at need of medical support.

In terms of impact on manufacturers, this product can provide work opportunities in environmentally safe conditions. Because this product will be made of biological materials and no synthetic materials, this ensures that all materials used will be compostable and safe to handle around humans. This promotes a sustainable production process and environment for manufacturers.

Moreover, the blanket design can be widely used among different genders and races during an emergency state where the user is in need of physical comfort, sense of protection and warmth. However, the jacket version of the design is more applicable to "western" cultures and groups of people. The jacket design can be difficult to wear for groups of people who wear long robes or elaborate coverings on a day to day basis due their culture and tradition. Lastly, our team aims to create accessibility in order to make this product available to everyone. This means that by making it very affordable, provided by medical workers to patients, available in stores or with fast shipping, and in different sizes, our team aims to make this solution available to many groups of people.

Sustainability

The blanket and/or jacket is made out of biological materials which requires less materials and produces non-toxic waste during production. They are also able to degrade under certain conditions when the product is no longer needed. Biotextiles are also more compatible with a diverse range of skin types. Lastly, the dual form of this product, a blanket or jacket, reduces the amount of products users need to prevent, detect, and respond to hypothermia.
